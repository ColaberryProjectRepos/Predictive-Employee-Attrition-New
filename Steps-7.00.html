ROI Comparison 7

Case 1 - Identifying High-Risk Departments for Employee Turnover

  • Strategy: Department-focused workload optimization, project reallocation, and enhanced recognition programs. 

  • Savings: 10 employees retained x $30,000 (cost to replace) = $300,000 savings.

  • Cost of Strategy Implementation: $100,000 for workload adjustment and project reallocation for 100 employees.

  • ROI Calculation: ($300,000 - $100,000) / $100,000 = 200%.


Case 2 - Impact of Salary Increases on Retention

  • Strategy: 5% salary increase for employees in the lowest salary band.

  • Savings: Retaining 10 employees avoids the $30,000 replacement cost per employee, totaling $300,000 savings.

  • Cost of Strategy Implementation: $200,000 for a 5% salary increase across 100 employees.

  • ROI Calculation: ($300,000 - $200,000) / $200,000 = 50%.


Both Case 1, with its focus on workload optimization and recognition programs, and Case 2, centered on salary increases for lower-band employees, demonstrate effective strategies for boosting employee retention. Case 1 achieves a remarkable 200% ROI by saving $300,000 in potential replacement costs for just a $100,000 investment, highlighting the value of targeted departmental interventions. Case 2, while offering a lower ROI of 50%, still underscores the critical role of fair compensation in retention, saving the same amount in replacement costs with a $200,000 investment. Together, these cases exemplify how tailored approaches to employee satisfaction and financial incentives can lead to substantial organizational benefits, both in morale and in economic terms.
